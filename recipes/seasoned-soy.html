Seasoned Soy Sauce

Image Credit: imma.eat.chu)

What is this dish?

This is not a dish on it's own but rather a dipping sauce/condiment/marinade!

It adds extra flavor to your ordinary soy sauce - everything from tofu, meat, veggies, dumpling, noodles, eggs -- you get the point.

You just need a few essential Korean pantry items to complete this delectable sauce. It tastes salty, sweet, savory, nutty, and even a little spicy if you want. It's typically served as a dipping sauce for fried foods like dumplings, Korean pancakes or spooned over tofu, veggies, rice! It has so many uses, I can't even write it all out. I keep a little jar of it in my fridge for whenever I want to add some additional flavor to my meals.

Hope this sauce serves you well!

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1-3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1-3 tablespoons seasame oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon seasame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon crushed garlic
  • 2 chopped green onions
  • 1/2 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 teaspon sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 chopped serano pepper (optional for more spice)

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, combine all of the ingredients and enjoy! Easy peasy
  • Tip: The longer you leave it in the jar, the more flavorful it gets!
